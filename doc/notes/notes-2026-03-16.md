Here's why the three Fmt m specializations are genuinely distinct:

Fmt Builder           — flat accumulation (no layout decisions)
Fmt (Tree m ann)      — structured documents (layout decisions deferred)

You never actually need Fmt (Fix (Cons chunk)) as a formatter, because Fix (Cons chunk) is the output — it's what lazy ByteString/Text already are. Builder is how you produce it efficiently. The recursion schemes enter when you want to consume or transform the output:

                            Fmt level                    Fix level
                            ─────────                    ─────────
  Simple formatting:   Fmt Builder a b  →  Builder  →  toLazyByteString
                                                        (= Fix (Cons chunk))

  Pretty-printing:     Fmt (Tree m ann) a b  →  Tree m ann
                                                    │
                                                refold (layout)
                                                    │
                                                Fix (Cons token)
                                                    │
                                                fold (render)
                                                    │
                                                Builder → Fix (Cons chunk)

  Consuming output:    Fix (Cons chunk)  →  fold/unfold/refold/hoist
  (lazy BS/Text)       (recursion schemes applied directly, no Fmt)

The unification is at Fix, not at Fmt:

- Fix (FmtF m ann) — trees (branching, layout-sensitive)
- Fix (Cons token) — token streams (linear, layout-resolved)
- Fix (Cons chunk) — chunk streams (what lazy ByteString/Text are)

All share the same fold/unfold/refold/hoist. And the streaming metamorphisms from cirklon apply to the Fix (Cons _) stages — so yes, you get recursion scheme access to lazy ByteString/Text for
free, because they're already Fix (Cons chunk) under the hood.

The one place where things could collapse: if you don't need width-sensitive layout, Fmt Builder and Fmt (Tree m ann) are redundant — just use Fmt Builder. The tree only earns its keep when you need group/flatten/align. Fmt m picks the right m for the job, and Fix f with recursion schemes handles everything downstream.
